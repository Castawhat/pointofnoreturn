The point of no return

i’m rowan. i know that names don’t mean much here, but i keep one for the sake of remembering who i am.
trench is huge. they never mentioned how big it really is in dema.

every step carries a weight. i stumble across marks left by others — scratches, symbols, warnings — yet somehow they guide me.

the further i go, the more the place feels alive. i notice carvings: numerals and shapes, letters interlaced like a code. 
only careful eyes catch them. they seem to spell out fragments of a phrase.

inside dema, they practiced this thing called vialism. i’m not sure what it is, but that’s why i escaped.
i saw the battle of paladin strait.

—rowan
