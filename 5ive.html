The point of no return

i left the small clearing before dawn, the mist curling around my ankles.
the path ahead was narrow, but familiar markings guided me.

i moved four kilometers up, counting every step, each one heavier than the last.
then, i turned — two kilometers to the left, following a faint trail of carvings.
finally, i pushed forward three kilometers north, past twisted roots and crumbling stones, until i reached a hollow marked by an old tree stump.

something waits there. a pattern. a sequence. a direction.
if you’ve come this far, follow my steps. trust me.

east is up,
—rowan
